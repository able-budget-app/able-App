The Q2 Roadmap

Drafted 2026-04-27 · Owner: Paul · Trigger: Plaid pay-as-you-go + Kelty audit

The short version

The next ~10 weeks lock the product down so the marketing stops chasing it. Bank sync was the trigger.

Once bank sync, the analyzer, the tax bucket, the named method, the brand, and the tour are all set, the marketing gets a single coordinated rewrite. The 175 social posts, the blog drafts, the landing page, the emails, the ads — they all get rewritten once instead of every time the product changes.

The order matters: build the product, lock the brand, update the reference doc, then rewrite the marketing. Skip a step and you redo the work.

Where things stand — May 12

Phases 1, 2, and 3 are done. Phase 4 is mostly done. The product is feature-complete: bank sync, analyzer, tax bucket with quarterly projections, MFA, web push, deep-dive on first connect, in-app monthly-to-annual upgrade, multi-bank with newly-added-bank review modal (shipped May 11). Floor-First method is named, written, in-app. Brand is locked. Reference doc is up to date.

Marketing is most of the way there. Landing page redone (May 7). Method explainer page live. All 14 email templates redesigned plus 2 new ones (May 7). PYF blog cluster (10 articles) is published, pending a small voice sweep (the trial-day CTA card needs 7d → 30d). Business blog cluster shipped per the original scoped plan (pillar + 1 supporting). 175 social posts ready and the cross-poster is wired. YouTube auto-upload pipeline live (May 10). LinkedIn pipeline ready with 13 drafts in the sheet pending review. Open marketing gaps: ad copy (not started), video script refresh (not verified), email cron auto-fire status (cron exists, schedule may need enabling).

What's next: ad copy, SEO engine, full social-cadence go-live, then App Store. Launch hardening (Phase 4.5) closed May 14 — error tracking, daily digest, feedback button, status banner, and Launch funnel are all live. Plaid-driven money in/out (B6) is parked until real customers tell us what they expect.

02The order matters

This whole plan exists to stop one specific kind of rework. Marketing copy written before the product is locked has to be rewritten when the product moves. Every redo costs a day or three. The order below is engineered so each layer locks the one below it.

1. Product capabilities
↓
2. Brand spec — logo, colors, type, motion
↓
3. The reference doc — what Able actually does
↓
4. Landing page, in-app tour, in-app copy
↓
5. Long-form: blog posts, video scripts
↓
6. Short-form: social posts, ads, emails
↓
7. Always-on engines: SEO, social cadence
Skip a layer and you rewrite the layers below.

05What not to do

    
Specific rework traps. Each one is a thing we've already burned time on or seen close calls.


    

      

        Don't do this
        Do this instead
      

      
Rewrite blog posts before the reference doc is updatedLock the reference doc first, then write once

      
Run ads at one price, then change the priceReconfirm pricing before the marketing rewrite starts

      
Shoot product video before the UI polish is doneWait for Phase 2, then capture in Phase 3

      
Promise things the product doesn't actually doMatch the copy to what shipped

      
Name the method after writing content with generic termsPick the name in Phase 0

      
Build household/partner sharing in the middle of bank-sync workDefer to Q3, revisit then

      
Turn on the social poster against stale post captionsWait for the Phase 4 caption sweep

      
Tweak the logo, colors, or type during the content rewriteLock the brand. Freeze. Don't touch.

      
Re-export all 175 social posts because a color changedLock the colors before the marketing rewrite

07Things still to think about

    
Open questions where the answer changes the plan. Numbered for quick reference.


    
    
      
  1. Bank sync scope — comfortable with reading balances and transactions only for v1? Auto-marking bills paid when Plaid sees the charge adds about two weeks plus the headache of "did the user actually pay it or did Plaid get confused."
    2. 
      
  2. Plaid cost ceiling — at what per-user cost does Plaid stop being economic at $14.99/mo? Need a guardrail (e.g. throttle refresh rate above some threshold).
    3. 
      
  3. Manual entry path — keep it after bank sync ships? Recommendation: yes. Default to "connect a bank" but keep manual as a visible option for privacy-conscious users.
    4. 
      
  4. Tax bucket build vs surface — is the existing tax bucket already doing the quarterly math, or just acting as a savings bucket? If the math is there, this is a UI job not a build job.
    5. 
      
  5. The method name — your call. Options: "The Able Method," "The Floor Method," "The Five-Bucket Rule," "The Reservation Method." Or something fresh.
    6. 
      
  6. The five rules — first draft to react to:
        
      
          
    • Know your floor — bills plus tax equals the amount you can't miss
      • 
          
    • Every deposit fills the floor first — before anything else gets a piece
      • 
          
    • Pay yourself before extras — smoothing reserve comes before free spending
      • 
          
    • Build the cushion before the splurge — a month of floor in reserve first
      • 
          
    • Score what happened, not what you intended
      • 
        
    
      
    7. 
      
  7. Pricing tiers — open to a Starter ($7.99 manual-only) / Pro ($14.99 with bank sync, analyzer, tax) / Founder ($99 lifetime) split later? Phase 5+ thinking, but tiering changes how the paywall code works so worth deciding early.
    8. 
      
  8. Marketing freeze — pause paid ads and new content from when Phase 1 starts until Phase 4 ships? Recommendation: yes, except retention emails and existing organic social.
    9. 
      
  9. Other writers' work — keep going during the freeze? Recommendation: pause new drafts until the reference doc is updated. Existing drafts are fine to hold but don't publish.
    10. 
      
  10. Cross-poster — leave it paused per the pivot memo? Recommendation: yes, until the Phase 4 caption sweep is done.
    11. 
      
  11. Audit rejections — anything in the "reject" or "defer" column you actually want to fight for?
    12. 
      
  12. Brand changes before lock — anything you already know you want to change? Wordmark, colors, type, motion? Or freeze with no major edits?

How to use this doc

      
Daily. Open it at the start of a session. Find the current phase. Pick the next thing that isn't done.

      
Weekly. Update the status section at the top. Move items between phases if the scope has shifted.

      
When tempted to start something out of order. Re-read the "what not to do" section. If it skips a layer, don't start.

      
When new ideas show up. Add them to a parking lot, not the current phase.

      
This plan is a living doc. Edit it. But don't skip the phases.
