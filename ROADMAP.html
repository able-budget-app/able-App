The Q2 Roadmap

Drafted 2026-04-27 · Owner: Paul · Trigger: Plaid pay-as-you-go + Kelty audit

TL;DR

The next ~10 weeks are about locking the product so we stop rewriting marketing. Plaid is the trigger.

Once Plaid + Analyzer + Tax + Methodology + Brand + Tour are locked, the existing 175-piece social library, the paused 9-article blog cluster, the landing page, the email sequence, and the ads all get a single coordinated rewrite — instead of a rolling rewrite every time the product moves.

The discipline: product → brand → capabilities skill → content. In that order. Anything that violates that order creates rework.

02The dependency principle

Why this doc exists. If you write at any layer before the layer above is locked, you rewrite. Every loop back up costs 1-3 days. The whole sequence is engineered to stop that.

Product capabilities
↓
Brand spec (logo, color, type, motion)
↓
Capabilities skill (single source of truth)
↓
Landing + tour + in-app copy
↓
Long-form content (blog, video scripts)
↓
Short-form (social, ads, emails)
↓
Distribution engines (SEO, social cadence)
Each layer locks downstream. Skip a step and you rewrite.

        Do this instead

  2. Plaid pricing ceiling — what's the per-user cost ceiling at which Plaid stops being economic at $14.99/mo? We need a guardrail (e.g. "if a user costs >$3/mo in Plaid fees, throttle their refresh rate").
    3. 
      
  3. Manual entry path — keep it after Plaid? My rec: keep, default to "connect bank" but make manual a visible option for the privacy-first crowd.
    4. 
      
  4. Tax elevation — is the existing taxes bucket already doing quarterly projections, or just acting as a savings bucket? If it's already capable, this is a UI surfacing job not a build job.
    5. 
      
  5. Methodology name — your call. Seed options: "The Able Method," "The Floor Method," "The Five-Bucket Rule," "The Reservation Method." Or something brand-new.
    6. 
      
  6. Methodology rules — sketch them now? Straw man:
        
      
          
    • Know your floor (bills + tax = the unmissable amount)
      • 
          
    • Reserve before you decide (every deposit fills the floor first)
      • 
          
    • Pay yourself before extras (smoothing/buffer come before free spending)
      • 
          
    • Build the buffer before the splurge (one month of floor in reserve before discretionary)
      • 
          
    • Score what you did, not what you planned (monthly score = what actually happened)
      • 
        
    
      
    7.
