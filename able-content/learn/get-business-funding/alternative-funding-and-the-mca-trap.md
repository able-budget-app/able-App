# Alternative funding and the MCA trap

Fast money comes at a brutal cost

When banks say no, alternative lenders fill the gap. Some are legitimate. Others are predatory products that can destroy a business.

The most dangerous is the Merchant Cash Advance (MCA). Structured as a purchase of future receivables, MCAs sidestep state usury laws. APRs hit 70 to 350%.

Here's the lay of the land.

## Key points

- <strong>MCAs are the most expensive money you can borrow.</strong> Factor rates 1.1 to 1.5 mean a $50K advance requires $55K to $75K repayment. APRs up to 350%. No discount for early payoff.
- <strong>Daily ACH withdrawals destroy cash flow.</strong> MCAs pull 10 to 20% of daily sales automatically. Accounts drain before payroll. MCA defaults hit $2.22B in 2024, up 59%.
- <strong>Business lines of credit are the smart alternative.</strong> 8 to 30% rates. You only pay interest on what you use. Fundbox, BlueVine, bank lines much safer.
- <strong>Invoice factoring works for B2B.</strong> Unpaid invoices from good customers? Factoring advances 70 to 90% immediately and collects from the customer. Fees 1 to 5% monthly.
- <strong>Equipment financing stays attached to the equipment.</strong> The equipment secures the loan, so rates are reasonable (8 to 20%) and approval is easier.

## The MCA rule of thumb

If a lender pitches speed, daily payments, factor rates, or personal confessions of judgment, walk away. Red flags for predatory terms. Cheaper options exist if you plan ahead.
