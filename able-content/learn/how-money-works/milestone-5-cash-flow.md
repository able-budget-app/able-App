# Milestone 5: Cash flow

The gap between earning and spending

Cash flow is the most important number most people don't track. Income minus expenses = freedom. Bigger gap, more options. Every dollar in that gap pays off debt, builds savings, or invests for you.

Most people don't know their real cash flow. Subscriptions hide. Food spending drifts up. Variable income makes averages hard to see. The goal isn't a budget. It's awareness.

Two levers matter most: money in and money out. You can work on either. Usually need both.

## Key points

- <strong>Try the 50/30/20 rule.</strong> 50% of income on needs, 30% on wants, 20% on savings and debt payoff. Simple starting guideline.
- <strong>Pay yourself first.</strong> Move savings and debt payments before anything else when income hits. Reverse budgeting. Flips the usual order (bills, wants, savings last) so your future actually gets funded.
- <strong>Watch for silent leaks.</strong> Subscriptions, auto-renewals, small daily charges. $10/day is $3,650/year. Run the math on every recurring charge.
- <strong>Boost income where you can.</strong> Side gigs, raises, skill upgrades, hobby into income. Cutting expenses has a floor. Growing income doesn't.
