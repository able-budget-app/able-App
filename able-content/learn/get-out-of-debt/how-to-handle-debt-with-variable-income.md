# How to handle debt with variable income

The missing playbook for entrepreneurs

Every debt book assumes steady income. If yours isn't, those plans break by month three. You need a different approach.

Plan in two modes. Floor mode for slow months: protect the essentials. Surge mode for good months: throw everything at your target debt.

Able is built around this. That's why you're here.

## Key points

- <strong>Build a buffer first.</strong> Stack one full month of bills in a separate savings account before aggressive debt payoff. Slow months won't force you back onto cards.
- <strong>Know your floor.</strong> The exact monthly minimum to cover every debt. Never go below it.
- <strong>Surge on good months.</strong> Big check? Most of the surplus goes to your target debt. Not lifestyle. Not catching up. Straight to debt.
- <strong>Review every 30 days.</strong> Variable income means the plan flexes. Check your floor, buffer, and debt progress once a month. Adjust.
