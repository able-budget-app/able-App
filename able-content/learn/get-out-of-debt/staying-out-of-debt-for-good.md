# Staying out of debt for good

The habits that prevent relapse

Staying out of debt is harder than getting out. Most people pay it off and end up back within 3 to 5 years.

The difference isn't discipline. It's systems. People who stay out build guardrails that make slipping back nearly impossible.

These are the habits that keep you free.

## Key points

- <strong>Keep your buffer funded.</strong> It's what stops slow months from becoming credit card months. Never spend it to zero.
- <strong>Pay cards in full every month.</strong> Can't pay the full statement? You can't afford what you bought. This one rule prevents 90% of debt.
- <strong>Track net worth monthly.</strong> Total assets minus debts. This one number tells you whether you're moving forward, regardless of income.
- <strong>Leave room for guilt-free spending.</strong> Deprivation budgets fail. Build spending money in. Able does this on purpose.
- <strong>Talk about money with someone you trust.</strong> Secret financial lives lead to secret spending. A monthly 15-minute check-in cuts relapse risk.
