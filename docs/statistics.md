# Able statistics source-of-truth

The canonical reference for every stat used in Able content. Every blog post, social caption, ad, email, and video script pulls from this file. If a number changes, update it here first; everything downstream re-cites from here.

**Last refreshed:** 2026-05-14
**Owner:** Paul Johnson
**Voice rules:** No em dashes. No emojis in copy. Use exact phrasing in the "Cite as" line for consistency across channels.

---

## Citation discipline

1. Every Able use of a stat MUST link to or reference the source.
2. Use the exact phrasing in the "Cite as" line. Slightly different phrasings across surfaces confuse the reader and weaken topical authority.
3. Always include the year of the data when it improves credibility ("87% in 2026" beats "87%").
4. If a number is older than 24 months, flag in the "Notes" field. Update when newer data is available.
5. Never round in a way that inflates. 33% stays 33%, not "nearly 40%."

## Tier 1: The hero stats

These are the strongest, freshest numbers. Lead with these on landing pages, ads, hooks, and pillar article openers.

### Stat 1 — 77 million Americans get paid different amounts every month

- **Source:** Federal Reserve, *Economic Well-Being of U.S. Households in 2022* (released May 2023)
- **Source URL:** https://www.federalreserve.gov/publications/2023-economic-well-being-of-us-households-in-2022-income.htm
- **Exact figure:** Nearly 3 in 10 adults (~30%) reported income that varied month to month. Applied to ~258M US adults, this is approximately 77 million.
- **Cite as:** "Nearly 1 in 3 American adults, about 77 million people, get paid different amounts every month."
- **Date pulled:** 2026-05-14
- **How to use:** Headline number for "scale of the problem." Use in landing-page hero, pillar article opener, hero ad copy, the ChatGPT/Perplexity-bait paragraph.
- **Notes:** Source data is 2022; this is the most recent Fed report covering month-to-month income volatility specifically. Refresh when 2024 SHED report drops.

### Stat 2 — 87% of Americans struggle to manage their spending

- **Source:** LendingTree, Financial Outlook Survey (January 2026)
- **Source URL:** https://www.lendingtree.com/debt-consolidation/financial-outlook-survey/
- **Cite as:** "87% of Americans say they struggled to manage their spending in 2025."
- **Date pulled:** 2026-05-14
- **How to use:** Universal-pain hook. Pair with a Floor-First reframe ("This isn't a discipline problem. It's a structure problem"). Works on top-of-funnel social, paid ads, email subject lines.

### Stat 3 — 88 million Americans say they are struggling or in financial crisis

- **Source:** Ramsey Solutions, *State of Personal Finance in America Q4 2025*
- **Source URL:** https://www.ramseysolutions.com/budgeting/state-of-personal-finance
- **Cite as:** "34% of Americans, about 88 million adults, say they are struggling or in crisis with their finances."
- **Date pulled:** 2026-05-14
- **How to use:** Authority hook. Quoting Ramsey supports the "extending Ramsey's principles to inconsistent income" positioning (do not frame anti-Ramsey).

### Stat 4 — Only 13% of Americans feel confident about their finances

- **Source:** Capital One Mind Over Money / StudyFinds reporting
- **Source URL:** https://studyfinds.org/money-worries-just-13-of-americans-feel-confident-about-their-finances/
- **Cite as:** "Only 13% of Americans feel very good about their finances."
- **Date pulled:** 2026-05-14
- **How to use:** Aspiration gap. Pair with the Able promise ("Calm is a feature, not a side effect"). The 87% inverse is the addressable market.

### Stat 5 — 60% of freelancers worry about managing irregular income

- **Source:** Remote, survey of 3,000 business owners, freelancers, and employees (US + UK)
- **Source URL:** Referenced via [Upwork gig economy statistics](https://www.upwork.com/resources/gig-economy-statistics)
- **Cite as:** "60% of freelancers worry about managing irregular income."
- **Date pulled:** 2026-05-14
- **How to use:** Persona-specific hook for freelancer-targeted copy. The cleanest "this is exactly the Able problem" stat.

## Tier 2: Supporting stats by topic

### A. Income volatility and inconsistent pay

**A1. Wage earners specifically: 33% report variable monthly income**
- Source: Federal Reserve SHED 2022
- Cite as: "33% of wage earners say their monthly income varies, compared to 13% of those on non-labor income."
- Use: Comparison stat. Shows it is a labor-market phenomenon, not retiree noise.

**A2. 89% of households experience a month-to-month income change of 5% or more**
- Source: JPMorgan Chase Institute, *Weathering Volatility*
- Source URL: https://www.jpmorganchase.com/institute/all-topics/financial-health-wealth-creation/report-weathering-volatility
- Cite as: "89% of US households experience month-to-month income changes of at least 5%."
- Use: The "everyone has volatility, even W-2 workers" frame. Note: 2013-2014 data. Older but still the strongest "month-to-month" measurement on record because it used actual bank-account data, not self-report.

**A3. Median household sees 36% income swing month-to-month**
- Source: JPMorgan Chase Institute, *Weathering Volatility*
- Cite as: "The median US household sees its income swing 36% month to month."
- Use: Concrete swing magnitude. Pairs with Floor-First explanation.

**A4. 31% of households see year-over-year income swings of 25%+**
- Source: JPMorgan Chase Institute

## Headline number recipes (ready-to-use combos)

### "Universal pain → Able is the answer" combo
> 91.8% of Americans worry about their budget. Only a third have one that works. 77 million of them get paid different amounts every month, and every budgeting app on the market was built for someone who doesn't.

### "Identity scale" combo (for freelancer/creator copy)
> 70 million Americans work in the gig economy. 27.7 million of them are full-time independents. 60% say they worry about managing irregular income. Able was built for the 60%.

### "Confidence gap" combo (for top-of-funnel)
> 87% of Americans struggle to manage their spending. Only 13% feel confident about their money. The gap is not a discipline problem. It's a structure problem.

### "Floor-First reframe" combo (for landing page)
> 89% of US households see their income swing month to month. The median household swings 36%. No monthly budget survives that. Floor-First Budgeting routes every deposit the moment it lands, so the swings stop being your problem.

### "Ramsey-extension" combo (for SEO / authority)
> Dave Ramsey's principles work for steady paychecks. 77 million Americans don't get one. Floor-First Budgeting extends those principles to inconsistent income.

## Stats that are NOT in this doc on purpose

- Income-claim statistics for ads (FTC liability; Able cannot promise specific dollar outcomes).
- Anything from non-cited "X% of people..." blog posts without a clear original source. If we can't trace it to a survey or government data set, it doesn't get cited.
- Stats older than 2018 unless they are the canonical reference (JPMorgan Chase Institute month-to-month data from 2013-2014 is the exception because it remains the strongest measurement on record).
