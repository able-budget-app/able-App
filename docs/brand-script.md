# Able Brand Script

**Status:** v1.0, locked 2026-04-28
**Use:** Single source of truth for messaging. Every blog post, ad, email, video, social post, and landing block pulls from this file. Locked phrases are verbatim. Do not paraphrase.

**Voice rules (strict):**
- No em dashes. Hyphens are fine. Use periods, colons, line breaks, or parens for breaks.
- No emojis in product or marketing copy.
- Customer is the hero. Able is the guide. No founder POV. No "we" or "our team."
- Short sentences. Plain language. Warm, calm, specific.
- Second person ("you") in marketing. Able-as-guide voice in product UI.

---

## 1. CHARACTER

**Who:** Entrepreneurs with inconsistent income. Freelancers, creators, and business owners are the sub-types used in segmented content (social, ads, blog headlines). The umbrella label is "entrepreneurs."

**What they want:**
A tool that helps them budget and manage their inconsistent income.

That is the singular want. The three outcomes that follow (debt down, savings up, stop living unpredictable check to unpredictable check) belong in the Success section.

**Product statement (single sentence, use wherever a one-liner is needed):**
> "An app built for entrepreneurs who struggle to manage inconsistent income."

**North Star (the why-we-exist line):**
> "We help entrepreneurs budget inconsistent income so they can pay down debt, save, and stop living unpredictable check to unpredictable check."

**Audience taglines (interchangeable by context, all locked):**
- "Built for inconsistent income."
- "An app built for entrepreneurs who struggle to manage inconsistent income."
- "For freelancers, creators, and business owners."
- "Built for the paycheck that does not come every two weeks."

---

## 2. PROBLEM (4 layers)

### External (the visible obstacle)
Income doesn't arrive on a schedule. Every standard budgeting tool and piece of advice assumes a steady paycheck.

Locked:
- "Most budgeting apps assume a steady paycheck."
- "Your paycheck does not follow a schedule."
- "Inconsistent income makes budgeting feel impossible."

### Internal (what it feels like)
You freeze. The unknown of when the next check arrives makes you afraid to touch the money you have. So it sits. And sitting money leaks.

Locked:
- "You freeze."
- "Money that sits, leaks."
- "Money that sits doesn't stay put."
- "You don't move the money because you don't know when the next check is coming."
- "A little here. A little there. Nothing feels like a decision."

### Philosophical (the injustice)
This shouldn't take more discipline. The system was built around income people don't have.

Locked:
- "The problem was never you. It was the advice you were handed."
- "No one taught you how to budget income like yours."
- "You were never taught how to budget a paycheck that doesn't follow a schedule."
- **"You don't need more discipline. You need a plan built for income like yours."** *(the bridge line. Use whenever moving from problem into solution.)*

### Villain
The monthly-budget paradigm. The assumption that everyone gets paid on the 1st and the 15th. Not YNAB. Not Ramsey. Not any one app. The whole frame.

The villain's signature move is the Day 1 to Day 31 cycle. Use the verbatim escalation, no day labels with persona names attached:

> Day 1: Money comes in. Just in time.
> Day 7: A snack here. A subscription there.
> Day 14: The leak begins.
> Day 30: "Where did that $1,000 go?"
> Day 31: Shame. Guilt. Avoidance.

**The insight (most load-bearing line in the brand, reuse in many places):**
> "Entrepreneurs don't fail to pay down debt because they don't want to. They fail because they're afraid of not knowing when the next check is coming in."

This line is a candidate for hero-quote treatment on the landing page, ads, blog pull-quotes, video voiceover openers, and email subject derivatives.

## 3. GUIDE (Able)

**Empathy (lead with this, every time):**

- "This isn't your fault."
- "No one taught you how to budget income like yours."

**Authority (earn the right to lead):**
- Built specifically for inconsistent income.
- Per-deposit allocation, not monthly.
- Floor-First Budgeting methodology.
- A Coach that knows your real numbers, not generic advice.

Locked:
- "Built for the way you actually get paid."
- "Generic finance advice doesn't know your situation. Able does."
- "Same foundation. Different clock." *(positions Able alongside classic personal finance, not against it. Anti-Ramsey framing is OFF-brand.)*

## 4. PLAN

### Process plan (3 steps the user executes)
1. **Log what came in.** Ten seconds. Any amount. Any source. No categorizing.
2. **Able tells you where it goes.** Bills, debt, savings, free spending. Tap each move off as you do it.
3. **Debt drops. Savings grow.** Every check-off pushes your score up. The fear of the next check fades.

### Methodology (Floor-First Budgeting)
Where the *how* lives. Defined ultra-clearly the first time it appears in any piece of content. Canonical at `docs/floor-first-method.md`. Anchor points for the brand script:
- Floor = bills + tax (the unmissable amount)
- Every deposit fills the floor first
- One month ahead = Able

Floor-First lives in this Plan slot only. Customer-facing soundbites stay in Problem, Guide, Success, and Transformation.

### Agreement plan (the trust + risk removal)
- 30-day free trial. Long enough to receive a deposit, allocate it, and see your first monthly score.
- Card required. Cancel anytime.
- Same access on monthly or annual.
- Two months free on annual.

**"Become Able" is NOT a CTA.** Buttons and CTA links are always action-clear: "Start your free trial," "Start your 30-day free trial," or "Start free." "Become Able" lives in the Transformation slot as a closing tagline.

**Direct CTA (button labels, action-clear):**
- "Start your free trial"
- "Start your 30-day free trial"
- "Start free"

**CTA blocks (value statement + direct CTA, the strong pattern):**

- "30-day free trial. Experience Able through one full income cycle."

**Transitional CTA (low-commitment, for the not-yet-ready):**

- "See the system"

**Microcopy under every CTA (always present):**
- "30-day free trial. Card required. Cancel anytime."

## 6. FAILURE STAKES

The leak compounds. Every month they wait is $500 to $1,000 not put toward debt, savings, or next month's bills.

Locked:
- "By the 30th, $500 to $1,000 is gone."
- "The cost of waiting compounds."
- "The cost of starting is $14.99."
- "Six months of waiting equals $3,000 to $6,000 you didn't save."
- "A year equals up to $12,000 you didn't use to pay down debt."
- "Nothing changes if nothing changes."
- "Day 31. Shame. Guilt. Avoidance. You promise yourself next month will be different. But we know it won't."

**From → To pairs (use as bookends on long-form pages):**

**The verbs (the customer's new vocabulary):**

- I am capable.
- It is predictable.

## How to use this script

- **Long-form assets** (landing pages, blog pillars, video scripts, email sequences) hit at least 5 of the 8 sections in arc order.
- **Short-form assets** (ads, social posts, push notifications) pick ONE section and lean hard.
- **Locked phrases are verbatim.** Do not paraphrase. The wording is the brand.
- **Arc order:** Problem → Permission (guide empathy) → Plan → Stakes → Success → Transformation. Do not skip Permission.
- **Floor-First lives in the Plan slot only.** Soundbites stay in Problem, Guide, Success, and Transformation.
- **Positioning:** Not anti-Ramsey. Not anti-YNAB. The villain is the monthly-paycheck paradigm, not any specific competitor.
- **CTAs are always action-clear.** "Start your free trial." Never "Become Able."

## Appendix: Personas (internal use only, not customer-facing)

These three personas drive segmentation, ad targeting, and customer research. Do not use the labels in customer-facing copy. The Day 1 to Day 31 narrative IS these personas in motion, expressed without titles.

- **The Freezer** maps to Day 3 to Day 7. "You freeze. You don't touch it. You tell yourself it's safer untouched."
- **The Leaker** maps to Day 14 to Day 30. "The leak begins. Lunch. Gas. A subscription you forgot about."
- **The Shame Cycle** maps to Day 31. "Shame. Guilt. Avoidance. You promise yourself next month will be different."

When a customer reads the Day 1 to Day 31 narrative, they self-identify with the layer that fits them. No persona label required.
