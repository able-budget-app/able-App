# Profit First → Floor-First: Research & Mineable Hooks

Research synthesis on Mike Michalowicz's *Profit First* book and methodology, filtered for what's transferable to Able's Floor-First Budgeting method, brand voice, and content strategy. Compiled 2026-04-29.

---

## 1. Method snapshot

Profit First is a cash-management system for small business owners built on one inversion: GAAP's `Sales − Expenses = Profit` is replaced with **`Sales − Profit = Expenses`**. Every deposit lands in an Income account, then is distributed by percentage into 5 base accounts: **Profit, Owner's Pay, Tax, Operating Expenses, Income**. Optional 6th/7th accounts cover Materials & Subcontractors and Inventory. Two accounts (Profit "Hold" and Tax "Hold") are recommended at a *different bank* to add friction.

Allocations happen on a fixed rhythm — the **10th and 25th** of each month — and a quarterly **Profit Distribution** pulls 50% of the profit account as an owner dividend, leaving 50% as retained earnings. Targets follow **Target Allocation Percentages (TAPs)** scaled by Real Revenue tier — e.g. a sub-$250K business targets 5% Profit / 50% Owner's Pay / 15% Tax / 30% OpEx, scaling to 25% / 5% / 15% / 55% above $5M. Users start with whatever they can stomach (usually 1%) — the **Current Allocation Percentages (CAPs)** — and step toward TAPs annually.

## 2. Sticky language inventory

Phrases Michalowicz coined or anchored that became sticky:

- **"Sales − Profit = Expenses"** — the inversion. Most-quoted line. Reads like math, lands like philosophy.
- **"Profit is not an event. Profit is a habit."** — official tagline. Reframes a noun as a verb-shaped behavior.
- **"Take your profit first."** — imperative, 4 words, action-shaped.
- **"Cash-eating monster to money-making machine."** — book subtitle. Animal-to-machine before/after.
- **"Use small plates."** — applied Parkinson's Law. Borrows weight-loss meme; instantly visualizable.
- **"Parkinson's Law of money."** — demand for a resource expands to match supply. Authority-borrowing.
- **"Out of sight, out of mind."** — justifies separate-bank accounts.
- **"Revenue is vanity, profit is sanity, cash is king."** — not original to Mike but he reanchored it.
- **"The survival trap."** — the cycle of growing sales to escape pain, then expenses inflate to match.
- **"Bigger monster."** — what you build when you grow without profit.
- **"Real Revenue."** — Sales minus materials/subs. Lets percentages stay portable across business types.
- **"Cash-flow triage."** — used by both fans and critics; positions PF as ER medicine.
- **"The 10th and 25th."** — two dates as a brand. Calendar-as-product.
- **"Profit-generating machine."** — end state.

**Public-domain enough to riff on:** Parkinson's Law, small plates, primacy effect, debt snowball, envelope budgeting (predates him by a century). NOT YNAB's "every dollar gets a job" — that one is off-limits as of 2026-05-12, see #9 below.

**Avoid copying:** "Sales − Profit = Expenses," "the survival trap," "cash-eating monster," "TAPs/CAPs."

## 3. Behavioral philosophy

Profit First rests on five psychological claims:

1. **Parkinson's Law applied to money** — supply creates demand. If $10K sits visible in checking, you'll find $10K of expenses. Floor-First's per-deposit split inherits this directly.
2. **Primacy effect** — what comes first gets the most weight. Moving profit to the front of the math gives it the attention budget.
3. **Out-of-sight friction beats willpower** — separating accounts at a different bank uses physical friction. Floor-First's auto-rollover and per-bill reservations are the same mechanic, software-native.
4. **Small wins build identity** — start at 1%, not 10%. Confidence is a percentage too.
5. **Sequence is the real product, not the math** — `Sales − Profit = Expenses` is identical to GAAP; only the *order of operations* changes behavior.

**Where Floor-First aligns:** every-deposit splitting, friction-based behavior change, micro-percentage onboarding, sequence-over-math.

**Where Floor-First extends:** Profit First assumes a *business* with cycles predictable enough that a 10th/25th calendar works. Floor-First operates on **inconsistent income** — every deposit is its own moment, no calendar required. Strict superset.

**Where Floor-First contradicts:** Profit First's last bucket is Operating Expenses (residual). Floor-First's last bucket is **Free Spending** — meaning the residual goes to *the human*, not the business. That's the freelancer/creator inversion: their business *is* them.

## 4. Audience overlap

| | Profit First reader | Able persona |
|---|---|---|
| **Job title** | Small business owner, agency owner, contractor, e-com store, $250K–$5M revenue | Freelancer, creator, 1099 contractor, solo consultant, side-hustler |
| **Pain** | Profitable on paper, broke in reality; tax surprise; can't take owner pay | Money lands and disappears; tax panic in April; no buffer feeling |
| **Books read** | Reads business books, listens to entrepreneur podcasts | Reads productivity/self-improvement; might not identify as "entrepreneur" |
| **Bookkeeping** | Has QuickBooks; may have a bookkeeper | Has a checking account and Notion |
| **Tax structure** | Often LLC/S-Corp; quarterly estimates | Schedule C, mostly; quarterlies aspirational |
| **Trigger event** | Tax bill they can't pay; second year with no owner pay | First $5K deposit they blow through in 11 days |

**Overlap (~30%):** the agency-of-one, the Etsy seller doing $80K, the freelance dev billing $150K who never set up an LLC. These people read Profit First, try the 5 accounts, give up after 6 weeks because the 10/25 rhythm doesn't fit their deposit pattern. **They are pre-qualified Floor-First customers.** Multiple critics confirm most readers don't sustain the system.

**Divergence (~70%):** the Freezer, the Leaker, the Shame Cycle don't think of themselves as running a "business." They think of themselves as broke creative people. PF's marketing — "Cash-Eating Monster" — does not speak to someone who feels like *they* are the monster. That's Floor-First's whole opening.

## 5. Brand/content playbook (how Profit First grew)

The flywheel:

1. **Authentic-voice trade book first.** *The Toilet Paper Entrepreneur* (2008) was the wedge — irreverent, profane, memorable. Built the audience that bought *Profit First* (2014, revised 2017). **Voice was set before the methodology.**
2. **One book = one named system.** Each book is a methodology with a noun: Profit First, the Pumpkin Plan, Clockwork, Fix This Next, Get Different, All In. He doesn't write "books"; he ships *named operating systems*.
3. **Certification as the moat.** Profit First Professionals (PFP) trains accountants/bookkeepers in 3 tiers (Builder / Master Builder / Designer) — ~6 months of training, monthly fees, 500+ members in 25 countries. **The book is lead gen for the cert; the cert is the recurring revenue.** Advisors evangelize the book to clients.
4. **Podcast as long-tail funnel.** "Entrepreneurship Elevated" / "Mike Up In Your Business" plus heavy guest appearances. Mission line: **"eradicate entrepreneurial poverty."** A villain (poverty), not a feature.
5. **Repeatable book scaffolding.** Penguin/Portfolio "Entrepreneurship Simplified" series. Same cover system, same chapter structure (story → principle → action), same author voice. He turned himself into a category.
6. **Free resources page** as email-list catcher. PDFs, calculators, instant-assessment quizzes — every funnel pulls into PFP.
7. **Conferences + community.** Annual PFP conference; pods/mastermind groups; quarterly live training with members.

**What's mineable for Able:**
- Name the method (done — Floor-First Budgeting). Treat it like a product, not a phrase.
- Build a free **Floor Score** quiz/assessment — PF's "PF Assessment" is their #1 lead magnet equivalent.
- Future certification: "Floor-First Coaches" for accountants serving creators is a v3 moat, not v1 noise.
- Name a villain: Mike has "entrepreneurial poverty." Able could have **"the freelancer cliff"** or **"the 11th day."**
- A book — even self-published — would 5x the methodology's surface area.

## 6. Mineable hooks for Able

Specific drafts, not directions. Steal verbatim or remix.

1. **The inversion line.** PF: *"Sales − Profit = Expenses."* Able: **"Income − Floor = Free."** Same shape, different math, different audience. Pin to the homepage above the fold.

2. **Habit framing.** PF: *"Profit is not an event. Profit is a habit."* Able: **"Stability is not a salary. Stability is a habit."** Reframes what stable income actually is for someone who'll never get a paycheck.

3. **Apply Parkinson's Law cleanly (public domain).** Headline: **"Money expands to fill the account it sits in. Floor-First gives every dollar a smaller room."** Small-plates idea translated for software, not bank accounts.

4. **Counter the "cash-eating monster" with the human inversion.** PF treats the business as the monster. Floor-First flips: **"You're not bad with money. Your money has no job."** Speaks directly to the Shame Cycle persona — the monster is the *system,* not them.

5. **Calendar-as-product, but deposit-native.** PF branded the 10th and 25th. Able's equivalent: **"Every deposit is your 1st."** Or: **"You don't budget monthly. You budget per deposit."** The freelancer-native rhythm PF can't do.

6. **Name the villain.** PF has "entrepreneurial poverty." Able's villain: **"the freelancer cliff"** — the moment you go from "rich" to "rent's late" in 11 days. Or alternative: **"the 11th day."** Use in headlines, ad copy, opening lines of blog posts.

7. **"Triage" reframe (use critics' words against PF).** Critics call Profit First *"the pre-wedding crash diet of business finance."* Able's positioning: **"Floor-First isn't a diet. It's how you eat now."** Permanent operating system, not crisis intervention. Differentiates without naming PF.

8. **Five buckets, named like a person not a CFO.** PF's accounts read like a balance sheet: *Profit, Owner's Pay, Tax, OpEx, Income.* Able's already read like a life: *Taxes, Bills, Smoothing, Debt, Free.* Lean in. Tagline candidate: **"Five buckets. Every deposit, split."**

9. **The "every dollar gets a job" lineage — OFF-LIMITS as of 2026-05-12.** YNAB's phrase. Earlier guidance here said "safe to riff on" — Paul reversed that. Hard ban in customer-facing copy: the exact phrase and close paraphrases ("every deposit has a purpose", "money has a job", "what job does this dollar have"). Use Floor-First language instead: **"Every deposit is split the moment it lands."** "Split" + "deposit" + "the moment it lands" carries the same operational meaning without the YNAB lineage.

10. **One-line method spine (for landing/about/podcast intro):** *"Floor-First is the budgeting method built for income that doesn't show up on the 1st. Every deposit is split into five jobs the second it lands — taxes, bills, smoothing, debt, free spending — so the floor under your life is set before you've had a chance to spend it."*

---

## 7. Positioning gap

Profit First sits in the office of a $1M agency owner; Floor-First sits in the pocket of a $60K freelancer. Profit First treats the business as the patient and the owner as the doctor; Floor-First treats the human as the patient and the software as the nurse. The two methods share the same DNA — split every deposit, use friction not willpower, sequence beats math — but the audience, the rhythm (per-deposit, not 10th/25th), the residual bucket (Free Spending for a person, not OpEx for a business), and the emotional register (calm, not zealous) are different products.

**The clean line:**

> **"Profit First is for the business that won't pay its owner. Floor-First is for the human whose business is themselves."**

That positioning lets Able be cited *alongside* Profit First in podcasts and blog posts without ever needing to attack it — the strongest position to be in, because Profit First's 175,000+ existing users include exactly the freelance-cliff personas it was never designed to serve.
