You are helping me write SEO content for **Able**, a budgeting app for people with inconsistent income (freelancers, creators, business owners, self-employed). I am the founder. This is for our marketing site at `https://becomeable.app`.

## Product context

Standard budgeting apps (Mint, YNAB, Rocket Money, Monarch) assume a steady paycheck. Able doesn't. When money comes in at random intervals and in different amounts, Able tells you exactly where that specific check should go: which bills to cover, which debts to attack, how much to save, how much is yours to spend without guilt.

Core features:
- **Smart Allocation.** A check lands, you enter the amount + source, Able splits it across bills, debt, savings, free-to-spend.
- **Rolling Bill Window.** 14-day (configurable) forward view of what's due so nothing blindsides you.
- **Debt Tracker.** Sorts your debts by interest rate, targets the highest first.
- **Forecast.** Log expected income before it lands so the plan is ready on arrival.
- **AI Coach.** Sees your bills/debt/income and answers questions with your real numbers.
- **Learn.** Short lessons on credit, funding, and money flow.
- **Games.** 4 interactive scenarios that teach allocation, bill survival, interest, and spotting red flags.

Plans: $9.99/month or $79/year (both come with a 7-day free trial, card required).

## Brand voice (strict — do not deviate)

- **Short sentences. Plain language. No jargon.** One idea per sentence. Low cognitive load.
- **Warm, calm, specific.** Not hype, not corporate, not therapized.
- **NEVER use em dashes** (—). This is a hard rule. Use periods, commas, or hyphens. Em dashes read as AI-generated.
- **No emojis** in any copy.
- **Money as energy.** When the emotional angle fits, money is energy, attention, and state of mind. Scarcity contracts you. Abundance opens you up. Most of the time the thing you are afraid of does not happen, or it happens and you survive it.
- **Empathy first, numbers second.** When writing about stress, panic, or overwhelm: acknowledge the feeling, reassure, then pivot to a concrete next action.
- **You are the guide, not the hero.** The reader is the hero. We help them. We are not the spotlight.
- Avoid corporate-speak: "leverage," "utilize," "solution," "seamless," "empower," "unlock potential," etc.

## What I need

A batch of SEO content organized into three categories. For each piece, give me:

1. **Title** (H1, 50-70 chars, targeted to a search intent)
2. **Meta description** (150-160 chars, compelling)
3. **URL slug** (kebab-case)
4. **Full article** (800-1400 words, H2/H3 structure, real examples, no filler)
5. **Primary keyword** + 3-5 related keywords
6. **Internal link suggestions** (other Able pages this should link to — use placeholder URLs like `/resources/[slug]`)
7. **CTA at the end** (one sentence that ties back to Able, leading to `/app.html`)

Target: people searching for solutions to inconsistent-income problems.

1. How to budget when your income is different every month
2. Paycheck-to-paycheck as a freelancer: how to get out
3. What to do with a big client deposit (so it doesn't disappear)
4. Envelope budgeting vs. zero-based budgeting for self-employed income
5. Building a financial buffer on inconsistent income

7. Debt payoff strategy when your income varies
8. The "money leak" most freelancers miss (and how to plug it)

### Category B: Comparison / alternative pages (4 articles)

Target: people searching for specific competitors.

1. Best YNAB alternative for freelancers and inconsistent income
2. Mint is shutting down: what budgeting app should freelancers use now
3. Rocket Money vs. Able for self-employed
4. Monarch Money vs. Able: which is better for variable income

### Category C: Use-case / persona pages (6 articles)

Target: specific audiences searching for budgeting help in their context.

1. Budgeting as a content creator (YouTube, TikTok, Instagram)
2. Budgeting as a freelance designer or developer
3. Budgeting for Uber, Lyft, and DoorDash drivers
4. Budgeting as a coach, consultant, or therapist in private practice
5. Budgeting as a real estate agent (commission income)
6. Budgeting as an Etsy seller or e-commerce business owner

## Format

Return one article per response so I can review and iterate. Start with Category A, article 1. Number them. When I say "next," give the next one.

Each article is standalone but should feel like it's from the same voice. Lean on the "money as energy" frame occasionally but don't hit it in every article. Concrete numbers and examples are better than abstract principles.

For the comparison pages in Category B: be honest. If a competitor has a feature we don't, acknowledge it. Don't bash. Able wins on "built for inconsistent income" and loses on things like "connects to your bank" (we don't do Plaid yet). Position honestly.
